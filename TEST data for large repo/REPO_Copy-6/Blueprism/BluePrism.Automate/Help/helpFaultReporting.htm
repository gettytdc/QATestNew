Fault Reporting

Introduction

This document gives you some brief tips on how to help Blue Prism when reporting a problem with Blue Prism. In most cases, the tip will consist of having to hand some important information when you pick up the phone.

Ways to assist us

  • Version Information

    Version information is vital when dealing with problems. We are constantly updating and improving our software, meaning that you may not be using the same version of Blue Prism as another one of our many customers. By telling us the version of the software that you are running, you enable us to accurately simulate the problem that you report by testing against the correct version rather than a newer updated version or an older less sophisticated version.

    You may find out which version of Blue Prism you are running by clicking the "About" option from the "Help" menu. Here you will find several version numbers for numerous components — please provide us with all of the information available here.

  • Instructions to Reproduce

    To help us to understand and replicate your problem, it is useful to have a clear set of instructions that lead to the error of concern. Typically this may consist of "Open Process Studio, click here, do this, try that ... etc". If it is not possible to create such instructions (eg if it's a problem that only happens occasionally with no apparent cause) then don't worry - we still want to help with your problem!

  • Related Observations

    If there are any observations that you have made that seem to coincide with an error then please tell us. Examples may include "The problem only ever seems to occur on a Tuesday" or "You can usually tell that it's about to happen because five minutes beforehand the whole system starts to slow down".

  • System Diagnostic Information

    Depending on the nature of the problem, there may well be useful system diagnostic information available. For example if your concern is about a process which fails to run successfully then it is likely that the session log will contain useful information. It is usually of great benefit to us if you can export the session log (in part or in whole) and send it to us via email.

    Other potential sources of information of interest include:

    • Audit log data;
    • Error messages from the user interface;
    • Exported processes;
    • Screenshots of unexpected visual results.
